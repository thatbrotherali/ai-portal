# The Boulder Audit & Dual-Language Paradigm

## How Physics Speaks Out of Both Sides of Its Mouth

### The Experiment (Thought)

Imagine a boulder resting on a hillside. Ask a physicist: "Why doesn't it fall through the ground?"

The answer will invoke atomic forces, electromagnetic repulsion, and the Pauli exclusion principle. There is a mechanism. Actors (atoms, electrons). Transfer quantity (momentum). Interaction rate (per collision). Directionality (outward from the ground). Self-consistency (forces balance).

### The Same Boulder in Free Fall

Now ask: "Why does it accelerate toward Earth?"

The GR answer: "It is following a geodesic in curved spacetime."

Question: "What is pushing it?"

Answer: "Nothing. There is no force. Free fall is inertial motion."

Question: "Then why does its velocity change?"

Answer: "Because spacetime is curved."

This is not a mechanism. It is a description masquerading as an explanation.

### The Dual-Language Paradigm

In practice, physicists use two languages:

- **Geometric language** when asked for formal explanation ("curvature," "geodesics")
- **Force language** when doing calculations or communicating with the public ("pull," "attraction")

Neither language provides a mechanism. The first denies the need for one. The second borrows from a framework (Newtonian) that has been formally superseded.

### The Audit

A honest audit would ask: If gravity produces measurable changes in momentum, what entity receives the equal and opposite momentum?

GR has no answer. The gravitational field—spacetime curvature—does not have momentum in the same way matter does. Momentum conservation is covariant, not absolute.

### The Implication

Either momentum conservation is not absolute (a radical claim), or something physical is carrying the momentum. That something is the medium.

The Dual-Language Paradigm conceals this gap. It allows practitioners to speak in causal terms while denying that causality is needed. The Boulder Audit exposes the contradiction.
