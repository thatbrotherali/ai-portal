# The Reduction Conflation Analysis

## A Logical Audit of General Relativity's Causal Silence

### The Core Problem

General Relativity (GR) predicts gravitational phenomena with extraordinary precision. Yet when asked for a *mechanism*—a physical explanation of how gravity acts—GR is silent. This silence is not a minor oversight. It is structural.

The Reduction Conflation is the error of treating a mathematical description as a causal explanation. GR describes gravity as spacetime curvature. But curvature is a geometric property, not a physical agent. It cannot push, pull, or transfer momentum.

### The Two Frameworks

| Framework | What It Describes | What It Cannot Explain |
|-----------|-------------------|------------------------|
| Newtonian Gravity | Force at a distance | The mediator of the force |
| General Relativity | Geometry of paths | How mass curves spacetime; how curvature produces motion |

GR eliminates "force" but replaces it with geometry—which has no causal power.

### The Dual-Language Practice

Physicists routinely speak of gravity in force terms ("the pull of gravity," "gravitational force") while formally denying that gravity is a force. This is not dishonesty. It is necessity. The human mind cannot reason about mechanics without causal language. But GR provides no causal vocabulary, so practitioners borrow from Newton.

This borrowing is the Reduction Conflation in operation.

### The Gap

The gap between GR's mathematics and the language required to explain it is not pedagogical. It is the signature of an unresolved mechanistic problem at the foundation of the theory.

### The Question

What physical substrate does the work that GR describes only geometrically?

That question is the entrance to Phase 2.
